Hi there, and welcome to my personal website! I am Ajay Mandyam Rangarajan, currently working as a Senior Software Engineer in the GPU Meshing team at [Ansys/Synopsys](https://www.ansys.com/) in Germany.

Before joining Ansys, I spent four amazing years at [Dassault Systèmes](https://www.3ds.com/) in Darmstadt, Germany, across both software engineering and product management roles, with a focus on electromagnetic simulation software and CFD meshing.

Before Dassault Systèmes, I worked at RWTH Aachen University as a postdoctoral researcher with [Prof. Karen Veroy-Grepl](https://www.tue.nl/en/research/researchers/karen-veroy-grepl/). My research focused on reduced order modeling and parameter reduction. I earned my Ph.D. (Dr.-Ing) in Computational Sciences from RWTH Aachen University in 2021, where I focused on anisotropic meshing for higher order methods, particularly in external aerodynamic applications, with [Prof. Georg May](https://scholars.duke.edu/person/georg.may). My [Erdős number](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Erd%C5%91s_number) is 4.

Since 2025, I have been spending some focused time on technical AI safety (mostly research during free time). One of the best finds for this avenue was the [Apart Sprint hackathons](https://apartresearch.com/sprints). You can find some of our previous projects [here](https://apartresearch.com/project/sequential-cascaded-hamiltonian-neural-networks-4zzi) and [here](https://apartresearch.com/project/layerwise-development-of-compositional-functional-representations-across-architectures-1zgq). One such project ended up as a [blog on LessWrong](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/3zh4nnjoxDsknQYLw/enhancing-genomic-foundation-model-robustness-through%20#robustness%20#adversarialtesting). 

In parallel, I also fell in love with CrossFit which is definitely not a cult (it is, :D). I liked it so much that I built a small training dashboard of tracking my training [here](https://www.ajayrangarajan.com/dashboards/crossfit-training-dashboard.html). The CrossFit box I go to is super fun and the coaches and people there are amazing, reach out to me to learn more about it, and if you want, join me for a class.

More broadly, I am interested in questions around robustness and explainability in machine learning. These feel increasingly important as ML systems move into domains where mistakes are costly. Since January 2026, I have been an [Apart Research Fellow](https://apartresearch.com/news/explaining-the-apart-research-fellowships), where I work on technical AI safety problems in genomics with [Dr. Jeyashree Krishnan](https://www.jeyashreekrishnan.com/), [Dr. Andrea Loehr](https://anloehr.github.io/), and [Dr. Jason Hoelscher-Obermaier](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=FKrb_FwAAAAJ&hl=de). Starting in May 2026, I will also join [SAIGE](https://safeaigermany.org/) as a mentor for work on [LLM manipulation](https://safeaigermany.org/projects-spring26/ajay-jk-jason), co-supervising with Dr. Jeyashree Krishnan and Dr. Jason Hoelscher-Obermaier.

I have a passion for reading (mostly non-fiction), training for and running marathons and triathlons, and traveling to countries far away. You can find more about my adventures [here](https://armandyam.github.io/others/). Additionally, I enjoy automating various tasks and have a collection of interesting scripts and hacks. You can check out some of these projects in my [portfolio](https://armandyam.github.io/portfolio/).

I keep this website updated as much as I can with the latest news about my personal and professional projects, goals, and achievements.
