Hi there, and welcome to my personal website! I’m Ajay Mandyam Rangarajan, currently working as a Senior Software Engineer in the GPU Meshing team at [Ansys/Synopsys](https://www.ansys.com/) in Germany.

Before joining Ansys/Synopsys, I spent four amazing years at [Dassault Systèmes](https://www.3ds.com/) in Darmstadt, Germany, across both software engineering and product management roles, with a focus on electromagnetic simulation software and CFD meshing.

Before Dassault Systèmes, I worked at RWTH Aachen University as a postdoctoral researcher with [Prof. Karen Veroy-Grepl](https://www.tue.nl/en/research/researchers/karen-veroy-grepl/). My research focused on reduced order modeling and parameter reduction. I earned my Ph.D. (Dr.-Ing) in Computational Sciences from RWTH Aachen University in 2021, where I focused on anisotropic meshing for higher order methods, particularly in external aerodynamic applications, as a student of [Prof. Georg May](https://scholars.duke.edu/person/georg.may). My [Erdős number](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Erd%C5%91s_number) is 4.

Since 2025, I have been spending some focused time on technical AI safety (mostly hacky research, and once successfully a more advanced blog). One of the best finds for this avenue was the [Apart Sprint hackathons](https://apartresearch.com/sprints). You can find some of our previous projects [here](https://apartresearch.com/project/sequential-cascaded-hamiltonian-neural-networks-4zzi) and [here](https://apartresearch.com/project/layerwise-development-of-compositional-functional-representations-across-architectures-1zgq). One such project ended up as a [blog on LessWrong](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/3zh4nnjoxDsknQYLw/enhancing-genomic-foundation-model-robustness-through%20#robustness%20#adversarialtesting). In parallel, I also fell into CrossFit which is definitely not a cult (it is, and I love it). Here is my not-live training [dashboard](https://www.ajayrangarajan.com/dashboards/crossfit-training-dashboard.html) and my super cool box: [Blackbox Darmstadt](https://www.blackboxdarmstadt.de/training)

Outside of my professional life, I have a passion for reading (mostly non-fiction), training for and participating in marathons and triathlons, and exploring new places. You can find more about my adventures [here](https://armandyam.github.io/others/). Additionally, I enjoy automating various tasks and have a collection of interesting scripts and hacks. You can check out some of these projects in my [portfolio](https://armandyam.github.io/portfolio/).

I plan to keep this website updated with the latest news about my personal and professional projects, goals, and achievements.

Feel free to download my CV to learn more about my academic background, research interests, publications, and professional experiences.
